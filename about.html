Origin

Born from the
heart of the Andes

TAMPU was founded in 2012 by a group of ecologists, anthropologists, and indigenous leaders who shared a single conviction: that the survival of the planet's most vital ecosystems depends on the people who have cared for them for centuries.

The word Tampu comes from Quechua — the ancient Andean language — and means "resting place" or "waystation." For us, it represents the space where travelers meet, where knowledge is exchanged, and where the journey continues with renewed purpose.

Today we operate across six countries in Latin America, connecting ancestral wisdom with modern conservation science to protect biodiversity, restore degraded land, and empower the communities who are the true guardians of the earth.

Our Journey

A decade of change

2012

Foundation

TAMPU Project established in Lima, Peru, with a team of 6 founding members and a mission to protect indigenous ecosystems and knowledge systems.

2014

First Restoration Site

Launched the first corridor restoration project in the Madre de Dios region, partnering with three Shipibo-Conibo communities to restore 400 hectares of degraded forest.

2017

Ancestral Seeds Network

Created a network of 12 indigenous seed banks across the Andes, preserving over 2,400 varieties of native crops threatened by industrial agriculture.

2019

Regional Expansion

Extended operations to Bolivia, Ecuador, and Colombia. Forest Guardians youth training program reached 600 young people in its first year.

2022

10 Years · 2,000 Hectares

Celebrated a decade of work with the milestone of 2,000 hectares restored, 38 communities supported, and partnerships with 14 international conservation organizations.

2026

Living Water Initiative

Launched the Living Water project — protecting high-altitude water sources critical for both Andean ecosystems and the drinking water supply of 3 million people.
